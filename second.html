Be Kind. Make Time.

Welcome back to my second blog post! This - like the first blog post - has been a long time coming. There have been so many things I have wanted to write about. From week two of my internship at Tesla - when I was feeling pressure ramping up and wanted to discuss something - I don’t know what it was, but it was something deep and insightful (or at least I think it was). Likewise, as you’d imagine, I’ve learned a thing or two working at one of the fastest-paced companies in the industry and building some of the coolest things I’ve ever gotten the chance to work on, but I think it would only be fair to have another post dedicated to a reflection on my internship.

So the title of this post is Being Kind and Making Time. This has been an odd semester for me as you can imagine - or maybe you can’t because you don’t know that I’ve been working at Tesla full-time this semester as a software engineer intern and a part-time student (taking one business minor course). What’s really funny is I’ve always idolized the idea of a co-op or taking a semester off full-time. I thought, hey - wouldn’t it be cool to leverage the fact that I can graduate early to work for another company, gain some experience, and take advantage of all of the other benefits that come along with working for a company? And while working full-time as an SWE Intern has helped me hone my software development skills, it’s forced me to make changes in my life and has led to some realizations about myself and life as a whole.

And while working full-time as an SWE Intern has helped me hone my software development skills, it’s forced me to make changes in my life and has led to some realizations about myself and life as a whole.

Actually, to be fair, all of this probably isn’t so bad. I have a friend - a crazy one at that - who decided to work full time, take a full courseload of classes, and do all the other routine things of life, and they made it out alive (barely). And I’m sure there are plenty of other people who can do the same. But so what? Recently, I’ve realized it’s okay to feel like I am unable to do everything I was able to when I wasn’t working full time, even if it means my job challenges my extrovertedness, which I take pride in. It’s okay if adulting and working 8-hour days tire me out, whereas the other interns around me can work for 10 - 12 hours a day without getting fazed. It’s okay if I don’t live up to the expectations imposed upon me by my friends, organizations, and other individuals that may be stressing me out.

The old me would have eaten myself up for being “complacent” about not solving these issues. But what’s really interesting is when you physically don’t have the time to take on these extra responsibilities or tasks - you have to back down. As they say, time is the great equalizer. And you see, that’s when things become interesting. For the last several weeks, I have been frustrated about the fact that when I finish the week, I oftentimes have not reached to friend so and so, have not hit this many leetcode problems, and a consequently unprepared for my interview the following week, have not updated my client for the group project on what we’ve done, want to use the weekend to catch up on sleep, and yet feel like I am behind on my project at work. However, I can also very peacefully acknowledge all of these things as facts, be kind to myself, and move on with the understanding that I’ve put my best foot forward, and that’s all that matters. Hmm, what a brilliantly simple concept, right - one that I’ve probably preached to my friends a hundred times then they’ve told me about how they worked their butt off but still didn’t reach their desired result.

It’s all about perspective - mostly yours - that should dictate your life (preach Neal pt. 2). But really, I think if I let go of some of the things that I think are making my life miserable - like the potentially negative social perception of me if I leave an organization or the fact that I am too tired to reach out to make plans and may get labeled fake across a subset of my friend group - everything will be fine. I will live. And probably not burnout. LOL. That was a pretty weird ending, but this was kind of a PSA, self-validation type piece and something I wanted to write. Idk at this point, it’s 2:30 a.m. and I haven’t proofread this. I probably won’t get around to typing my annual custom Thanksgiving messages to the 75-100 people I am thankful for, so if you are reading this, I am probably thankful for you. I hope you have/had a good Thanksgiving and get to spend it with the people you love. Cheers and goodnight.
